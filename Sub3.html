Kids & Privacy

Website 1

URL https://www.ftc.gov/business-guidance/privacy-security/childrens-privacy/a>
Site Author US Government
Abstract The website "ftc.gov" helps businesses understand how to protect children's privacy online. It explains the rules they need to follow, like getting parental consent before collecting personal information from kids under 13. The site also provides tips on implementing security measures to keep children's data safe. By following these guidelines, businesses can ensure they're handling kids' information responsibly and legally. This helps protect children's privacy and creates a safer online space for young users.

Website 2

URL https://www.doi.gov/privacy/children
Site Author U.S. Department of the Interior
Abstract The website "doi.gov" helps keep kids' information safe online. It gives advice and tools for parents, teachers, and businesses to protect children's privacy. The site explains rules like COPPA, which makes sure companies handle kids' data responsibly. It also offers tips for parents and teachers to teach kids about staying safe online. By sharing this information, the website works to keep children's personal information secure and make the internet a safer place for them.
