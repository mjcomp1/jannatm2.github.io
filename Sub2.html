Child development and technology

Website 1

URL https://www.naeyc.org/resources/topics/technology-and-media/infants-and-toddlers
Site Author National Association for the Education of Young Children
Abstract The website "naeyc.org" offers valuable resources on the topic of technology and media usage among infants and toddlers. Through articles and practical guidance, the content explores the effects of technology and media exposure on early childhood development, focusing on the unique needs and vulnerabilities of infants and toddlers. It also emphasizes the importance of mindful and age-appropriate use of technology, providing recommendations for parents, caregivers, and educators on promoting healthy screen time habits and maximizing learning opportunities through interactive media experiences.

Website 2

URL https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC9273128/
Site Author National Library of Medicine
Abstract The website "ncbi.nlm.nih.gov" hosts an article about using technology to prevent and treat obesity in kids. This article reviews many studies to see how effective things like apps, wearable devices, and telehealth are in helping kids be healthier. It shows what works and what doesn't in using technology to fight childhood obesity. This information is helpful for doctors, researchers, and policymakers who want to find new ways to keep kids healthy. Overall, the article highlights the potential of technology to improve children's health and offers insights into better strategies for preventing and treating obesity in young people.
